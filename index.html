Reviewer calibration

Workshop flow

The deeper goal is reviewer calibration: helping people become more consistent, evidence-based, and constructive when assessing HDR proposals.

Set Up

Each group assesses one selected proposal. Everyone reviews individually before discussion, using the sliders and evidence boxes.

Individual Review

Each reviewer selects scores for the five criteria, writes evidence from the proposal, and completes: Strength, Concern, Required revision, Final recommendation.

Use Save Review before group discussion so your first judgement is preserved.

Small Group Discussion

Within each group, reviewers compare:

  • Which criteria received the widest score differences
  • What evidence each person used
  • Whether concerns are about proposal quality, fit, feasibility, novelty, or writing clarity
  • Whether the recommendation follows logically from the scores
  • What revision advice would be most useful to the candidate

Group Consensus

Each group then creates a shared final review:

  • Final score range
  • Final recommendation
  • Top 2 strengths
  • Top 2 concerns
  • Required revisions
  • Any unresolved reviewer disagreement

Whole-Room Debrief

Each group reports back for 5 minutes:

  • Proposal assessed
  • Average score and recommendation
  • Strongest criterion
  • Weakest criterion
  • Main evidence used
  • Final revision advice

What Groups Can Discuss Later

They can discuss whether reviewers interpreted the rubric consistently. For example, one reviewer may score “novelty” low because the topic is familiar, while another scores it higher because the Vietnam context is underexplored.

Useful discussion questions:

  • Did reviewers reward clear writing more than research strength?
  • Was “school alignment” judged by topic, method, field, or supervisor fit?
  • Did the evidence support the score, or was the score based on general impression?
  • Were “Weak Accept” and “Reject” used consistently?
  • What makes required revision actionable rather than vague?
  • Did the proposal’s field affect expectations of methodology or contribution?

Comparing Individual Reviewer Outcomes

Export each individual review using Save Review, then compare:

  • Scores by criterion across reviewers
  • Average score per reviewer
  • Final recommendation per reviewer
  • Evidence cited for each criterion
  • Strength/concern/revision comments
  • Difference between numerical score and written recommendation
